Learn the Metric System

The metric system is built on simple powers of ten. Once you understand how prefixes work, you can move between units by shifting the decimal instead of memorizing random factors.

1. Base units

In this app we use meters (m) for length, grams (g) for mass, and liters (L) for volume. Every other metric unit in that family is a scaled version of the base.

  • 1 m = 100 cm = 1 000 mm
  • 1 kg = 1 000 g
  • 1 L = 1 000 mL

2. Prefixes as powers of ten

Common prefixes simply tell you how many powers of ten away from the base unit you are:

  • milli- (m): 10⁻³ → 1 000 of them make the base unit.
  • centi- (c): 10⁻² → 100 of them make the base unit.
  • kilo- (k): 10³ → 1 of them is 1 000 base units.

When you convert between prefixes, you are really just moving the decimal left or right. The converter’s tip field will remind you how many places you moved it.

3. Metric vs. imperial thinking

Imperial units (feet, miles, ounces, gallons) usually require memorized factors like 12, 3, 16, or 8. The metric system keeps the base the same and only changes the prefix.

As you try conversions, focus on these questions:

  • Is the metric unit larger or smaller than the imperial one?
  • Should the numeric value get bigger or smaller?
  • How many powers of ten away from the base unit am I?
