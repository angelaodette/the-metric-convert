04 — Voice & Tone
Brand Voice
Smart
  • Explain the "why" behind metric, not just the "how"
  • Use real-world context — body temp, weather, cooking
  • Respect the reader's intelligence; don't talk down
  • Cite facts confidently without being preachy
Witty
  • Embrace the absurdity of Fahrenheit and feet
  • Light jokes are welcome — condescension is not
  • Wordplay earns its place when it also teaches
  • Self-aware: we were all confused once too
Bold
  • Make strong, clear claims — no mealy-mouthed hedging
  • Short sentences. Active voice. No filler.
  • Lead with the punchline, explain after
  • Make metric feel inevitable, not optional
Welcoming
  • Never shame imperial users — we're converting them, not judging
  • "You" not "one" — personal and direct
  • Celebrate small wins (you just thought in km!)
  • Meet people where they are, take them somewhere better
Tagline candidates
Finally, the system that makes sense.
From inches to insight.
Because the rest of the world already knows.
One conversion. Zero going back.
05 — Usage
Do's & Don'ts

Do

Use Bebas Neue for all display headlines and unit callouts
Pair Crimson with Chalk or Midnight for high contrast
Use Spark amber sparingly — labels, badges, and key stats only
Write in active voice with short, punchy sentences
Use Instrument Serif italic for quotes, taglines, and warmth moments
Let metric numbers be the hero — big, bold, centered

Don't

Don't use generic fonts like Inter, Roboto, or Arial
Don't use Deep Red (#7A0C0C) as a text color on dark backgrounds
Don't stack more than 2 colors in a single UI component
Don't use Spark as a primary color — it's an accent only
Don't mock or shame imperial users in copy
Don't use gradients — flat colors only, per brand identity
